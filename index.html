Recent jobs

Real rooms, real finishes.

A few from around Wolverhampton — colour chosen with the customer, lines kept crisp, mess kept off your floors.

Feature wall

A quiet two-tone that does the work.

A built-in media recess picked out against soft sage, with crisp white returns. The kind of detail that makes a whole room feel considered rather than just repainted.

Cutting in around recesses and angles is where a tidy hand shows — no ragged edges, no overspill onto the ceiling.

Through-room

Deep navy, kept clean against white.

An open-plan living and dining space carried through in a confident navy, with beams and ceilings left bright white so the colour reads as a choice, not a weight.

Big blocks of dark colour are unforgiving — every transition has to be sharp. This one holds up close.

Stairs & woodwork

A stairwell that sets the tone.

Walls in a warm teal with spindles and banister glossed clean — the first thing you see coming through the door, so it earns the care.

Stairwell decorating is awkward, high work. Done right, it's one of the jobs that changes how a whole house feels.
